Wellington's Internet Infrastructure

When we think of the internet we don't really think of it being something tangible instead we tend to think of it as a cloud. So through some exploration around our capital city i have compiled some of these tangible things.

Antennas

Antennas around Wellington that signal potential internet infrastructure.

Home Router

My home router has two antennas on top of it. This is so that it can recieve Wi-Fi signals at a greater data rate and align them according to where coverage is needed having a downstairs and upstairs.

Uni Router

There are many routers around campus which allow for smooth flowing Wi-Fi.

Signs

Signs around Wellington that signal potential internet infrastructure.

Chorus Live Cables

Chorus is a telecommunications provider throughout NZ. It is the owner of majority of telephone lines in NZ and builds approximately 70% of the new fibre optic Ultra-Fast Broadband network. Chorus has 4 broadband options: ADSL, VDSL, Fibre and Fibre Pro with 400,00 homes throughout NZ connected to Fibre.

ParkMate

In some car parks around Wellington there are signs where you can use internet and download the app ParkMate which you can use to pay for your parking.

Chromecast

Chromecast is a dongle which enables users with mobile devices or computers to have internet-streamed content playback through their TVs or home studio systems. Originally Chromecasting was supported by only these four apps: Youtube, Netflix, Google Play Music, Google Play Movies & TV. However since its launch this has expanded to 5,000+ app including: Neon TV, Spotify, SoundCloud, Facebook, Twitch, FanPass, NBA, WWE etc.

Utility Pole

Various cables connecting from utility poles to houses which can include communication and broadband lines such as fibre optic cables.
